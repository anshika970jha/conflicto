Educational Content

What is war?

War is a state of armed conflict between nations or groups, involving violence and often leading to destruction and long-lasting societal impacts. Types of war include interstate wars, civil war, guerrilla war and proxy wars.

Causes of War

War arises from:

  • Political disputes (control, ideology)
  • Economical factors (resource competition)
  • Ethnic/religious tensions
  • Territorial disputes (borders, land)
  • Nationalism (identity conflicts)

Educational Content

Consequences of War

  • Humanitarian Impact: loss of life, displacement, trauma, human rights violation.
  • Economical Impact: destruction of infrastructure, economical collapse, rebuilding costs.
  • Social Impact: displacement of people, cultural loss.

Role of International Organisation

  • United Nations (UN): peacekeeping and conflict resolution.
  • International Red Cross (IRC): Aid and humanitarian support
  • International Criminal Court(ICC): Prosecution of war crimes.

Educational Content

Conflict Resolution and Peacebuilding

  • Diplomacy: negotiation to prevent war.
  • Peace Treaties: official agreements to end hostilities.
  • Reconstruction: Rebuilding post-conflict societies.

Ethics of War

  • Just war theory: when is war morally justified?
  • Civilian Protection: the ethics of collateral damage.
  • Weapons of mass destruction: Ethical concerns around nuclear, chemical, and biological weapons.

Educational Content

Future of War

  • Cyber warfare: attacks on digital infrastructure
  • Drones and AI weapons: the rise of automated combat
  • Climate change: Potential for resources-driven conflict.

Conclusion

Understanding war's causes and effects is essential for preventing future conflicts. Diplomacy, international cooperation, and respect for human rights are key to achieve lasting peace
